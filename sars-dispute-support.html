Disputes are won or lost on the evidence trail

SARS disputes rarely turn on clever argument alone. Assessors and appeal bodies look for a coherent factual record: contracts, invoices, system data, operational documents and a clear link between the tax position and what actually happened in the business.

Meridian provides structured SARS dispute support in South Africa for businesses, tax teams and professional advisers who need calm, evidence-led strategy, not reactive paperwork.

Our approach to SARS disputes

  1. Separate the questions: legal, factual, procedural and commercial issues are not the same problem.
  2. Map the evidence: build a document and data trail that supports each element of the position.
  3. Draft with scrutiny in mind: submissions that are structured, provable and commercially sensible.
  4. Fix underlying weaknesses: where poor controls or records caused the dispute, address the system too.

Frequently asked questions

When should a business get SARS dispute support?

As early as possible, ideally at audit or query stage, before positions harden. Early support helps frame issues, map evidence and avoid submissions that sound strong legally but fail on facts.

What is the difference between a SARS objection and an appeal?

An objection is typically the first formal challenge to an assessment. An appeal follows if the objection is disallowed. Both require clear legal argument supported by evidence; the record and deadlines differ at each stage.

Does Meridian provide legal representation before SARS or the Tax Court?

Meridian provides advisory and project-based support, strategy, evidence mapping, drafting and preparation. Legal representation is only provided where expressly agreed in writing.

Can you support diesel refund or customs disputes specifically?

Yes. Meridian has particular experience where tax disputes intersect with fuel records, operational controls and customs/excise evidence. See our diesel refund and customs & excise pages.
